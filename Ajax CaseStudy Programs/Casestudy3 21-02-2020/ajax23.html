Bus is a clipped form of the Latin adjectival form omnibus ("for all"), the dative plural of omnis-e ("all").[4] The theoretical full name is in French voiture omnibus[1] ("vehicle for all"). The name originates from a mass-transport service started in 1823 by a French corn-mill owner named Stanislas Baudry [fr] in Richebourg, a suburb of Nantes. A by-product of his mill was hot water, and thus next to it he established a spa business
